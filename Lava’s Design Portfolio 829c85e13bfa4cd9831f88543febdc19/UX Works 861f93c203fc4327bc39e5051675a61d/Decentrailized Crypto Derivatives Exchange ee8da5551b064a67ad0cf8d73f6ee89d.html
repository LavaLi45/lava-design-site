Product Background:

If an user owns a single digital asset, the platform which we called Contingent Claims Platform(CCP) will allow the owner to split that digital asset into its constituent pieces (e.g. a Digital Asset = Long Call + Short Put) and create collateralized digital tokens representing each such exposure and offering those tokens to other users.

The platform inherently increases liquidity across the ecosystem by design. It is built such that the required payoffs will always automatically be made to the relevant parties in a transaction. No centralized intermediary is required to ensure payment or may intervene payment.

Challenge:

  • The concept is new in reality and there is fewer competitive products for reference.
  • Data is the key information needs to be organized for users to make better decisions.

1. Knowing The Users

A variety of people can be the potential users, such as asset managers, hedge funds, long-term investors, market-makers or risk-takers. After interview some of them, I have come out with the user persona for our product.

2. Define Information Architecture

Knowing users and know users' need are the key to define the features of the products and the information architecture. I collect the users' initial questions and feeling of our products, and provided the possibilities to solve the problems and try to reduce the negative feeling of the users.

According to the questions and possibilities we've collected, the user tasks and the features can be defined clearly.

Must: Deposit the balance

Core: Bid/Offer a payoff

Necessary: Withdraw the balance to the personal wallet

Good to have: Order history, Real time market price, Order depth chart

2. Sketching Wireframe

In order to reach an agreement of the user journey and wireframe efficiently, I decided to discuss with our whole team by sketching on the paper. It was useful that we focused on the essential issues rather than details and try to prove the user-flow works.

However, as we have defined the main information architecture and the user tasks, I still need to combine these tasks to make the whole experience smooth and efficient.

In order to learn more about the feelings and thoughts of the users when going through the whole journey, I decided to design 2 different layouts while they have the same information sections to test.

2 different layouts while they have the same information sections to test:

3.Mock Up Testing

When there was no reference to consider, the easy way to find out the suitable design solution is to test and get feedback from the users and stakeholders.

Using the components we had built before, I quickly drafted out 2 types of main screen with different layouts, then we invited people to test.

Version 1 - One screen solution

All information was in one screen without scrolling. Pairs & payouts were listed in a vertical view to adapt to the future requirement.Put Order book, Table & Order form in the same section.

Version 2 - Long Page solution

Users can scroll the screen to see more information.

More information have been show to help users to make decision: price&rate on the left, recent trade record, buy & sell order form display together.

4.Expert Evaluation

Both internal and external experts have been invited to test our design and receiving lots of useful feedbacks for improving the previous design.However, there are always pros and cons collected from the experts:

Version 1:

the information is too simple and it is hard for users to follow the correct path although the path was from left to right, top to bottom.

Version 2:

some information is less important and the path to reach a goal is too long and sometimes users may get lost.

5.Final Version 3

According to the feedback from the mockup testing and the experts, the experience have been improved with a clear layout and well-organised user journey.

  • Used dropdown to switch pairs because of the less frequency of switching pairs.
  • Gather the first 3 selectable menu, Select Pairs, Select payouts, Select Expiry, on the left panel.
  • Hiding information which is irrelevant with core task to the second view.
  • The path to complete the core journey is from left to right.
