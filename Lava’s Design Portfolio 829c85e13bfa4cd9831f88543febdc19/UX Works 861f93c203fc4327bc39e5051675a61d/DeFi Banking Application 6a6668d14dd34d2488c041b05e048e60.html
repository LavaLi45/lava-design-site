Product Background:

A key innovation of this product allows exchange Participants/Users to create and capitalize trustless asset and liability capital structures whereby a User can buy, sell, borrow, lend, and take views on specified digital assets. The Users can serve as market-makers or risk- takers in whatever way they see fit. Users can take digital asset or crypto-currency “deposits”, pay interest on these “deposits”, and then deploy these digital assets, in order to earn a return above their promised deposit rate.

The role of the Users is to specify a desired asset and liability capital structure and set a maturity date for that structure ( We will call this structure as a Bank ). This bank will be fully transparent to users in the ecosystem. Any user in this ecosystem can establish themselves as a Sponsor by funding their bank.

There are a variety of obvious candidates to be potential users across the digital asset universe, including asset managers, hedge funds, long-term investors, market-makers or risk-takers of any type. Importantly, the decentralized nature of this solution provides for the true democratization of digital asset credit creation, as well as transparency in terms of lending criteria.

1. Know competitive products

At the beginning, I have no ideas about the digital asset trading business. By researching quickly on the current relevant websites relating to lending, borrowing and exchanging, I got a general understanding of the crypto currency. I chose these 4 products to analysis because they were having a mature and clear product view in this area.

Compond:

Provide a way for people to earn interest instead of putting the majority of cryptocurrencies sit idle on exchanges and in wallets.

CDP Maker:

Can get liquidity by generating SAI stablecoins without giving up ownership of your collateral.

Oasis:

One of the first places for people to buy and trade Dai.

Celsius: Let users buy coins, earn interest on their crypto and instantly borrow dollars at 1% APR against it without fees ever.

After going through the core user flow of other products, I compared the user experience in different aspects, such as Core Functions, Interaction, UI style, Aha moment, Bad mood etc.

The analysis makes the design easier since it provided an overview for what will raise the great impression and what should be avoided to design in my product.
